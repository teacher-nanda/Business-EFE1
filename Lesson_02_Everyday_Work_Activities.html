📐 Grammar

2.1 Key Language — The Present Simple

Use the present simple to talk about things you do regularly, such as your daily tasks or everyday work routines.

📌 When to use the Present Simple

Use the present simple to talk about things that happen regularly as part of a routine.
"Every morning, we prepare the food and Justin sets the tables."
✅ Use it for:
  • Daily routines & habits
  • Regular tasks at work
  • Things that are generally true
  • Job descriptions
⏰ Common time expressions:
every day every morning on Mondays usually twice a day at 11am

📐 Grammar

2.2 How to Form the Present Simple

With regular verbs, use the base form with I, you, we, they. With he, she, it — add -s to the base form.

Subject Verb Rest of sentence
I / You / We / They prepare the food every morning.
He / She prepares the food every morning.
📝 Regular verbs (+s)
work → works
prepare → prepares
read → reads
eat → eats
⚠️ Special: "be" forms
I am (I'm)
He / She is (He's / She's)
We / You / They are (We're)
⚠️ Spelling changes
go → goes
do → does
have → has
finish → finishes
💡 Remember from Lesson 1: We use these forms to introduce ourselves too! "My name's Alisha." → "name's" is a contracted form of "name is".

💬 Speaking

Present Simple — Let's Practice!

Use what you just learned. Speak in complete sentences using the present simple.

✏️ My Job
Tell me about your job using the present simple. Say at least 4 sentences.
e.g. "I work in a marketing agency. I prepare reports every morning. I have meetings with clients. I usually finish at 6pm."
👩‍💼 My Manager
Tell me what your manager or boss does every day. Remember to use he/she + verb-s!
e.g. "My manager has a team meeting every Monday. She checks our reports. He travels a lot."
👥 My Team
Tell me about your team or colleagues. What do you all do regularly? Use "we" or "they".
e.g. "We have lunch together every day. They work very hard. We usually stop for coffee at 11."

📌 Lesson Review

Lesson 2 — Summary

Great work! Let's review everything you learned today.

📐 The Present Simple

  • Use for regular actions & routines
  • I / You / We / They → base form
  • He / She / It → base form + s
  • "be" → am / is / are

⏰ Time Expressions

  • every day / every morning
  • on Mondays / on Thursdays
  • usually / always / sometimes
  • twice a day / at 11am

📝 Key Verbs (work routines)

  • prepare · work · read · travel
  • eat · drink · leave · open
  • bring · walk · shout · stop
  • have · meet · check · write

🔗 Connecting L1 + L2

  • "My name's Alisha. I'm a manager." (L1 + be)
  • "I work at Spandone & Co." (present simple)
  • "Every day, I prepare reports." (L2)
  • Full introduction = who you are + what you do!
"Every morning, we prepare the food and Justin sets the tables. I'm always busy, but I love my job!"

Introducing Yourself

English uses a variety of polite phrases. The level of formality depends on the situation and the people you are meeting.

Greeting
Good morning. / Good afternoon. / Good evening.
Use time-specific greetings in professional meetings.
Greeting
Hi! / Hello!
"Hello" works in both formal & informal situations.
Name
My name is Alisha Sharma.
Full name — very professional. Use with clients & seniors.
Name
My name's Tom. / I'm Lee.
"Name's" = contraction. "I'm" = informal way to say your name.
Response
It's a pleasure to meet you.
Pleased to meet you.
"I'm" is implied before "pleased."
Response
Nice to meet you. / Good to meet you.
Great to meet you! → You too.
"You too" means "Great to meet you, too."
💡 Using Mr. / Ms. / Dr. + last name is very formal. First name only = informal. Always match your register to the situation!

💬 Speaking

Introducing Other People

You can also use polite formal and informal phrases to introduce your colleagues to each other.

🤝 Formal Introduction

"May I introduce Maria Diaz?
Maria is our sales manager for Europe."

→ "It's good to meet you, Maria."

"I'd like you to meet Zoe Carr."

→ "It's a pleasure to meet you, too."

😊 Informal Introduction

"Amit, meet Edward.
Edward, Amit and I work together."

→ "Great to meet you, Edward."

"This is my new assistant, Levi."

→ "Hi, Levi. Good to meet you."

💡 Use "May I introduce…" & "I'd like you to meet…" in formal contexts. Use "This is…" & "[Name], meet [Name]" with colleagues.

📚 Vocabulary

Choosing the Right Register

Register is the level of formality you use. Choosing correctly shows professionalism and social awareness.

📊 When to use each register
Very Formal
Client meetings 🤝 CEO / senior management 👔 Job interviews 📋
Formal
Business partners 🏢 Official emails 📧
Informal
Same-level colleagues 👥 Team chats 💬
🤝 Formal — Key phrases
My name is Sarah. (full form)
May I introduce…
It's a pleasure to meet you.
Good morning / afternoon.
😊 Informal — Key phrases
My name's Sarah. / I'm Sarah. (contractions)
This is… / [Name], meet [Name].
Nice to meet you. / You too!
Hi! / Hey!
💬 Speaking

Guided Speaking — Introductions

Follow the conversation step by step. Respond to each line using the phrases from this lesson. Use the hint if you need help.

👔 Scenario 1 — Very Formal
You are a new intern. It's your first day at a law firm. You meet the Senior Partner.
Ms. Rodriguez"Good morning. I'm Ms. Rodriguez, Senior Partner here. Welcome."
Your turn — Greet her and introduce yourself formally
"Good morning, Ms. Rodriguez. My name is…"
Ms. Rodriguez"It's good to meet you. Is today your first day?"
Your turn — Confirm and say you're happy to be there
"Yes, it is. It's a pleasure to meet you too."
Ms. Rodriguez"Excellent. Let me introduce you to the rest of the team."
Your turn — Thank her formally
"Thank you very much, Ms. Rodriguez."
🌐 Scenario 2 — Formal
You are at a company networking event. You meet David Chen, a professional from another company.
David Chen"Hello. I don't think we've met. I'm David Chen from TechPro."
Your turn — Introduce yourself and your role
"Hello. My name is… I work at…"
David Chen"Nice to meet you. Do you know anyone else here?"
Your turn — Introduce a colleague to David
"I'd like you to meet… She/He is my colleague."
David Chen"It's a pleasure to meet you both."
Your turn — Respond politely
"The pleasure is mine."
😊 Scenario 3 — Informal
It's your first week. A friendly colleague, Jake, comes to say hello in the open office.
Jake"Hey! You must be new — I haven't seen you before!"
Your turn — Say hi and introduce yourself casually
"Hi! Yes, I'm… I just started this week."
Jake"Great to meet you! What team are you on?"
Your turn — Tell him your team and ask about his
"Great to meet you too! I'm on the… team. What about you?"
Jake"Cool! Hey, meet my friend Ana from marketing."
Your turn — Greet Ana informally
"Hey Ana! Nice to meet you."
💻 Scenario 4 — Video Call
You join an international video call. Sarah, the meeting host, asks everyone to introduce themselves.
Sarah"Hi everyone. Let's do quick introductions. Who'd like to go first?"
Your turn — Introduce yourself to the group
"Hello everyone. My name is… I'm from…"
Sarah"Sorry, could you spell your last name for us?"
Your turn — Spell your name letter by letter
"Of course. That's [Name] — [spell it out]."
Sarah"Great, thank you! Nice to have you on the call."
Your turn — Respond and say you're happy to be there
"Thank you! Nice to meet you all."
Scenario 1 of 4
